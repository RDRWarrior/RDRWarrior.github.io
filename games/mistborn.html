Mistborn Platformer Prototype

A Unity-based third-person platformer exploring Allomantic Push/Pull mechanics as a core movement and combat system.

Engine Unity
Type 3D Platformer Prototype
Focus Movement Systems & Mechanics
Inspiration Mistborn / Allomancy

Overview

This prototype explores how Allomantic abilities from Mistborn—specifically steel Push and iron Pull—can function as the core of a platforming and combat system. The design centers on player interaction with the environment through visible connections to metal objects, enabling traversal, puzzle-solving, and combat through a unified mechanic.

My Role

I designed and implemented the core gameplay systems, including Push/Pull mechanics, movement flow, ability progression, and level structure. I also handled playtesting, iteration, and system refinement based on user feedback.

Core Systems

  • Steel Push: Propel away from metal objects or launch objects as projectiles.
  • Iron Pull: Pull objects toward the player or pull the player toward fixed anchors.
  • Metal Sight: Visual system highlighting interactable metal objects.
  • Pewter Enhancement: Boosts strength, jump distance, and combat effectiveness.
  • Resource System: Allomantic abilities designed around limited reserves.

Design Focus

The prototype focuses on unifying movement, combat, and puzzle-solving through a single interaction system. Push and Pull are not just abilities—they define how the player navigates space, interacts with enemies, and solves environmental challenges.

Level Design

The level is structured as a progressive sequence of suspended platforms above a hostile environment, with each section introducing and reinforcing a new mechanic.

  • Movement basics and Pewter-enhanced jumps
  • Steel Push vertical traversal
  • Iron Pull navigation and chaining
  • Combat encounters using the core mechanics
  • Final challenge combining all abilities

Iteration & Playtesting

Playtesting revealed key issues in difficulty, readability, and movement feel. Iterations focused on:

  • Reducing frustration through checkpoint systems
  • Fixing overly slippery movement via physics adjustments
  • Improving visibility of traversal paths
  • Refining input and Push/Pull usability

Technical & Design Challenges

The main challenge was designing a system where a single mechanic could support multiple gameplay roles without becoming inconsistent or unintuitive. This required balancing physics behavior, input responsiveness, and environmental design to ensure the mechanic felt predictable while still allowing emergent gameplay.

Outcome

The project demonstrates how a strong central mechanic can drive multiple gameplay systems simultaneously. It serves as a foundation for further development into a full game experience, particularly in combining traversal, combat, and systemic interaction.
